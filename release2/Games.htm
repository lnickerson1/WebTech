The Games of the 2019 NHL Stanley Cup Finals

Rules/Style

  1. Series/Game Style:
    1. The Stanley Cup playoffs consists of four rounds of best-of-seven series.
    2. The finals is the fourth and last series in the post-season. Games five, six, and seven are played only if needed.¹
  2. Home Ice Advantage:
    1. Each series is played in a 2–2–1–1–1 format, meaning the team with home-ice advantage hosts games one, two, five, and seven, while their opponent hosts games three, four, and six.
    2. In the finals home-ice advantage is awarded to the team that had more regular season points. In the case of the 2019 finals the Bruins had home ice advantage over the St. Louis Blues.
  3. Overtime Rules:
    1. "Unlike the regular season where a contest could eventually be decided in a shootout, overtime in the playoffs is played in multiple sudden-death, 20-minute five-on-five periods until one team scores."¹
  4. Basic Hockey Rules:
    1. A point is scored by shooting the puck into the opposing teams net. A player is not allowed to kick the puck into the net or purposely direct it into the net with any other body part.

    2. Each team has five skaters and one goalie on the ice at a time.
    3. NHL periods are games are played over 3 periods consisting of 20 minutes.
    4. Each play begins with a face-off and ends when the official blows their whistle or a goal is scored.
    5. For a list of penalities visit: Hockey Penalities.
    6. Offsides is called when a player enters the offensive zone before the puck crosses the blue line and then their team interrupts the play of the puck in the offensive zone (by touching it).
    7. Icing is called when a team shoots the puck from behind the red line to the opposing goal line without it touching anyone. This call is canceled if the team that shot the puck reaches the bottom of the offensive zone face off circles before the defending team.
