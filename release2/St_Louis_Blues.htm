Road to the Finals

2019 was the St. Louis Blues' sixth appearance in the Stanley Cup Finals. Their last appearance was in 1970, when the Boston Bruins swept them. Up until 2019 the St. Louis Blues had appeared in the finals four but lost all of them.¹ The Blues finished the regular season with 99 points putting them in third in the Central Division, fifth in the Western Conference, and twelfth in the league.³ In the post season the Blues defeated the Winnipeg Jets in six games in the first round. They then beat the the Dallas Stars 4-3. Finally they were crowned champions of the Western Conference after their six game series victory over the San Jose Sharks. They would then moved on to face the Boston Bruins in the finals.²


Home Arena

The St. Louis Blues home arena is the Enterprise Center, located in St. Louis, Missouri at 1401 Clark Ave, St. Louis, MO 63103. The Blues are the only sports team to use the arena however it is also a popular sight for concerts and other events. It has a capacity of 19,260 and was opened in 1994 under the alias of the Kiel Center.² It wouldn't come to be named the Enterprise Center until 2018.²
